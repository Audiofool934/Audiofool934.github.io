Spent the past few weeks building out the [Notes](/notes/) — 15 entries now, covering everything from [Markov chains](/notes/markov-chains/) to [flow matching](/notes/flow-matching/).

The exercise of writing these forced a distinction I hadn't articulated before: there's a difference between *understanding* something and *having a story* about it. A formula you can derive isn't the same as a formula you can explain to someone walking next to you. These notes are my attempt at the latter.

Some connections surprised me. The [policy gradient](/notes/policy-gradient/) note ended up referencing [martingales](/notes/martingales/) — the baseline trick in REINFORCE is really just a martingale difference argument. I wouldn't have seen that if I hadn't written both notes in the same week.

The format is deliberately terse: no textbook preambles, no "in this section we will discuss." Just the idea, why it matters, and where it connects.
